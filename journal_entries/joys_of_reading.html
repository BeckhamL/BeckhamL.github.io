Joys of reading

(April 22, 2024)

I was recently asked what was one good thing that came from the covid experience that positively impacted my life. The first thought that came to my mind was work from home. I mean c'mon, who isn't a fan of WFH? But giving it a little more thought, the best thing that came from covid for me was the joy of reading I discovered. One day as I was cleaning my room I found this book "A long way gone" by Ishmael Beah (fantastic read by the way) given to me by my parents years ago. As I picked up the book, a thought came to my mind that the last time I read a novel was literally grade 12 english. I debated for a while if I was actually going to read, but I'm so glad I did. The things I've gained these past 4 years from books have been invaluable not just in learning new things, but becoming a better public speaker and learning to be more empathetic to those around me.

My favourite books I have read:

  • Pachinko - Min Jin Lee
  • A fine balance - Rohinton Mistry
  • Brothers - Yu Hua

These 3 books are very sad. They all tell the stories of underprivileged characters from poor Asian countries who face wave after wave of struggles. But yet they keep moving forward. I hope as I look back on this journal entry years from now, I have added new books to this list and shared these novels with others.

I cannot remember the books I've read any more than the meals I have eaten; even so, they have made me - Ralph Waldo Emerson
